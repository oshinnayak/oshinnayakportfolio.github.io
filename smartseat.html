Designing for the Second half of Life

The Challenge: With a team of students from Art & Design, Business, Engineering and Information, work in a competitive product development environment to create a product with active technology for geriatrics, to help improve their health.
The Outcome: This project was part of the Integrated Product Design course at University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. This innovative course has been featured on CNN and written up in the New York Times, Wall Street Journal and Businessweek. By the semester my team and I had created a working prototype of a smart seat which was portable, foldable, and could give a slight boost while getting up.
Spoiler: The prototype was demoed at IPD 2018 Trade show and UMSI Exposition 2018 where about 100 people got to interact with it.
You can find some fun images from the IPD 2018 Trade Show here.
My Role: Product Designer
Team Members: Karthik Kota and Prachi Bhagane
Project Timeline: January 2018 - April 2018

Overview


The semester started with an abstract problem statement: Create a product with active technology to help geriatrics improve their health.

The range of health care issues geriatrics i.e. people over the age of 65 face are not just physical but also emotional and mental. The following sections explore how we ended up making a smart seat. We narrowed down our area of design intervention to "Independence" through the initial user research methods and brainstormed concepts which could tackle this issue.

From our brainstorming session we formed high level research questions:

  • How might we help them restore their independence?
  • How might we help to prevent falls?
  • How might we reinforce their importance in society?

These questions were further explored through personas, concept testing, and a conjoint analysis.

User Journey

Concept Testing and Analysis

Three main concepts which were tested

Memory Box: Allows users to celebrate and record their life story and connect across generations.

Portable Smart seat: Helps to easily get up from sitting position without anyone’s help. Compact and saves the space.

Shakey: Provides users with a fun way to build strength and boost their mood with music.

After skecthing out the prototypes and storyboarding them for our personas we moved on to making physical Prototyping of the concepts, we used cardboard and chocolates!



Choosing One Concept
